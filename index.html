Camel

  1. Camels are social animals who roam the deserts in search of food and water with up to 30 other individuals
  2. Camels’ ears are small and hairy. However their sense of hearing is also extremely strong.
  3. In Arab cultures the camel symbolises patience, tolerance and endurance.

Chameleon

  1. Chameleons are a very unique branch of the lizard group of reptiles.
  2. Chameleons live in warm varied habitats from rainforests through to deserts.
  3. Chameleons are not deaf but they do not actually have ear openings.

Cicada

  1. They’re true bugs (from the order Hemiptera). So you can call them a bug and be scientifically accurate.
  2. Cicadas make noise like toy frogs
  3. Cicadas have one of the longest insect lifespans

Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  2. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.
  3. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.

Teddy

  1. The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.
  2. The first British Teddy Bear Festival was held in 1989 in London.
  3. American multi-billionaire investor Paul Greenwood owned the largest collection of teddy bears.
